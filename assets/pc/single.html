Oliver also highlighted our legislative bureaucracy by discussing the failed attempts to give small businesses a permanent tax break for donating food. In February, the Fighting Hunger Incentive Act (HR 644) was proposed in the House of Representatives. By the time it passed in the House, it had been bundled together with other breaks and retitled the America Gives More Act. When it got to the Senate, where it was ultimately passed, the language to prevent food waste had been removed and replaced with language about border control and U.S.-Israeli relations. It was then retitled the Trade Facilitation and Trade Enforcement Act.
